# Agent Handoff — Pupa Counter (2026-04-30)

Supersedes `HANDOFF_2026-04-16.md`. The previous doc described the
v12 + clf_v5 + min_distance=8 pipeline at 99.46 % F1. This update
ships a strict improvement that does **not** retrain the CNN.

## What changed today

Two two-line edits in `pupa_counter.py`, plus one new file in
`model/`:

| File | Edit |
|---|---|
| `pupa_counter.py` | `PEAK_MIN_DIST = 8 → 5` |
| `pupa_counter.py` | `CLASSIFIER_PATH = .../peak_filter_clf.pkl → .../peak_filter_clf_v6_md5.pkl` |
| `model/peak_filter_clf_v6_md5.pkl` | New 380 KB GBM classifier, retrained on V12's md=5 peak distribution |

The CNN backbone (`model/pupa_counter_v12.pt`) is unchanged.

## Why

The 2026-04-30 inference-side investigation in
`pupa_counter_publish/scripts/sweep_inference.py` showed that V12's
peak extractor was *throwing away* good peaks. `peak_local_max`
applies a non-max suppression step controlled by `min_distance`; at
`min_distance=8`, any cluster-cousin peak within 8 px of a stronger
peak gets dropped — and that includes a lot of legitimate pupae in
dense clusters. Lowering it to 5 px:

- recovered 33 of 39 dense-cluster center misses;
- recovered 59 of 71 edge misses (still worth keeping);
- introduced 18 new false positives, because the legacy `clf_v5` was
  trained on the md=8 peak distribution and didn't know how to
  classify the new close-NN candidates.

So we retrained the GradientBoosting classifier on V12's md=5 peak
output (same 11-d feature vector, same 300-tree / depth-3 hyperparams,
same 0.60 cutoff). The new classifier, `peak_filter_clf_v6_md5.pkl`,
filters all 18 new FPs without losing any TPs. Net result on the full
99-scan / 10,145-label set:

| Pipeline | TP | FP | Miss (edge / ctr) | F1 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| v12 + clf_v5 + md=8 (legacy ship) | 10 035 |  0 | 110 (71 / 39) | **99.46 %** |
| v12 + clf_v6_md5 + md=5 (NEW ship) | 10 135 |  0 | 10 (6 / 4)    | **99.95 %** |

The honest leave-one-scan-out CV (`scripts/clf_v6_loo_cv.py` and
`scripts/clf_v5_loo_baseline.py` in `pupa_counter_publish`) confirms
the relative win:

| Pipeline | LOO F1 | LOO miss / 10,145 |
|---|---:|---:|
| v12 + clf_v5 + md=8 (legacy ship) | 99.08 % | 142 |
| v12 + clf_v6_md5 + md=5 (NEW ship) | **99.60 %** | 42 |

So real-world delta is **+0.52 pp** (3.4 × fewer errors per pupa),
not the optimistic 0.49 pp from all-on-all. Both classifiers have
the same ~0.35 pp overfit gap, so the ranking is honest.

## What is left in the 10 misses

- **4 interior misses** — all are dense clusters where two ground-truth
  centers sit 1–4 px apart. At `GAUSSIAN_SIGMA = 3` the heatmap merges
  those into a single peak; no choice of `min_distance` recovers both.
  This is an architectural floor and matches the ground-truth ambiguity
  zone (one labeled case has two centers 1 px apart, confirmed by
  Stephen as a real biological overlap).
- **6 edge misses** — half-cut pupae at the scanner border. Five of
  them have heatmap response > 0.6 at the label location, so the CNN
  saw them; one of them (scan 73, x=1113) has the closest *kept* peak
  230 px away, suggesting the classifier rejected the candidate on
  account of its near-edge geometry. A future iteration that adds an
  edge-aware classifier feature could likely save 2–3 of these.

## V13 attempt

A V13 run was also done overnight — fresh-init, v6 literal recipe
(uniform UP=1.0 / OP=0.5), 10,145 cleaned labels, 50 epochs. It
peaked at ep34 with F1 = 98.51 % / ne_F1 = 98.75 % on raw output, then
plateaued. Combined with its own retrained classifier
(`peak_filter_clf_v6_v13.pkl`, also in `pupa_counter_publish/tmp/`),
the full V13 pipeline got F1 = 99.93 %. That's **−0.025 pp behind the
v12 + clf_v6_md5** ship config — a tie, statistically.

The reason cleaner data did not deliver a CNN-backbone win: V12 carries
a **spatial UP weighting** (0.75 on the edge column, 1.5 on paper
interior) that V13's uniform recipe did not replicate. The 4 extra
edge misses V13 took (V13 edge=10 vs V12 edge=6) come from that
architectural difference; the cleanup gain on the interior side was
real but smaller than the edge gain V12 already had. V13 is on disk as
`tmp/cnn_checkpoints/best_v13.pt` (ep34 weights) for reference; not
shipping it.
